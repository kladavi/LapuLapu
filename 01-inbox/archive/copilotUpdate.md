Executive Summary
This week focused on converting strategic discussions into executable delivery plans across Batch Transition, Rapid Recovery (RRP), MMM L2, GOCC onboarding, and operational standardization. The most significant outcome was alignment on a formal GBO-owned 24x7 batch operating model, supported by a structured work breakdown, September pilot target, runbook governance, and observability requirements. Parallel progress continued on Rapid Recovery standardization, MMM L2 onboarding, dashboard maturity, and GOCC transition readiness. [mfc-my.sha...epoint.com]

Major Accomplishments
1. GBO Batch Transition Program Formalized
A comprehensive transition model was agreed to shift Japan batch operations toward a GBO-owned 24x7 operating model aligned with Lapu‑Lapu. Key outputs included:

Confirmation of centralized batch execution ownership by GBO.
Definition of seven work packages covering operating model, inventory, runbooks, execution readiness, pilot implementation, Ingenium alignment, and governance/reporting.
Establishment of a phased timeline culminating in a September pilot.
Enforcement of the principle“No Runbook = No Onboarding.”
Identification of critical risks around inventory completeness, ownership clarity, runbook quality, and 24x7 readiness. [mfc-my.sha...epoint.com]

2. Batch Operations Readiness Advanced
Follow-on discussions with GBO clarified supporting operational requirements:

ADX logging capabilities for batch troubleshooting were confirmed.
Sample runbooks and batch status report templates were provided for standardization activities.
