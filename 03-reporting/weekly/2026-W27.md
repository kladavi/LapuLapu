**Project:** Lapu-Lapu

Executive Summary

Frictionless Customer Experience: A production service account password expired on Saturday June 27 and cut off communication between the ING APIM platform and four Japan applications (SCV, MLK, PAW, SSW) until the password was reset. The account was supposed to be rotated every year but had not been touched since May 2024, no one was on record as its owner, and no reminder was ever sent — so it failed silently. This is very likely a systemic issue affecting other Japan service accounts in the same category, not just this one ID.

Robust Technical Core: CAP-48585 was raised to establish an estimation process for capacity management with the intent being to both prevent lack-of-capacity issues and right-size environments that are too big. The plan is now inside Lapu-Lapu, split into six work packages between GOCC and ETS, with Ingenium, NDM, and ServerF running as the pilot. Capacity risk will now be a standing item in Rapid Recovery Plans and the MMM L2 dashboards.

Technology Transformation through AI & Automation: The GBO Japan Batch Transition is on plan for the September pilot cutover, with the July onsite deployment still on track. No new decisions this week.

> Decision this week (D019): Adopt the CAP-48585 delivery plan as the way Lapu-Lapu executes the corrective action, targeting close by end of September.

Capacity Management (CAP-48585) [NEW]:
CAP-48585 was raised to establish an estimation process for capacity so we can both prevent capacity-shortfall incidents and right-size environments that are over-provisioned. The plan is split into six work packages — build a baseline of what's running today, describe the business demand drivers, forecast future demand, set traffic-light thresholds for when to watch, plan, and act, run monthly reviews with a quarterly forecast refresh, and publish results into a Power BI / New Relic dashboard tied to the enterprise risk rating. GOCC leads the baseline, forecasting, thresholds, and ongoing operations; ETS leads demand drivers and reporting. The pilot runs on Ingenium, NDM, and ServerF. Target close is end of September. [RISK] Data quality, gaps in monitoring coverage, and unclear application ownership are the top risks | Debamalya Das.

Credential Lifecycle Governance (INC08672078 / PRB00024864) [NEW]:
On Saturday June 27 the `mfcgd\wasAPIMprod` service account password expired and broke communication between ING APIM and SCV, MLK, PAW, and SSW. Password reset by GAM restored service later the same morning. Post-incident review with CyberArk operations found the account is in the "Semi-Managed" category — meant to be rotated annually by the password manager role — but no rotation has been done since May 2024, CyberArk was not configured to send pre-expiry warnings, and no accountable owner was on the record. T143 closes out the problem ticket with GAM and CyberArk operations: formalize the rotation cadence, fix the notification gap, add this failure mode into the affected Rapid Recovery Plans, and confirm the owner. T144 extends the review across the rest of Japan's Semi-Managed accounts to find any others in the same silent-expiry state.
