# Semi-Annual Project Status Report — Lapu-Lapu (2026 H1)

**Project:** Lapu-Lapu
**Period:** March 9 – July 3, 2026 (W11 – W27)

---

Executive Summary

Frictionless Customer Experience: H1 opened with a successful production monitoring go-live — 23 Japan applications (6 Gold and 17 Silver) were actively monitored inside the first week, with Ingenium set as the reference standard for full monitoring coverage. Over the following months the Employee Experience and Developer Experience dashboards moved from draft to shared with the business, Sales and other business stakeholders asked for direct access, and Ito-san opened a feedback loop asking for a business-friendly external health-check report. The branch monitoring pattern was extended from Japan to the Philippines. H1 closed with a serious reminder that the underlying platform is not free of hidden risk: a service account expired on June 27 and broke the ING APIM connection for four Japan applications until it was manually reset, exposing a gap in how "Semi-Managed" service accounts are rotated and owned across Japan.

Robust Technical Core: The half was defined by standing up Rapid Recovery as a real operational capability. The Ingenium plan became the first Gold-application plan built to the mandatory template, covering a full WAS → CICS → DB restart sequence and a batch-slowness workaround, and was published as a KB article. A server-restart authorization matrix was made mandatory in every plan after the June Ingenium freeze incident. In parallel, CMDB accuracy was pushed as the substrate for every downstream workstream (transition, MMM L2, patching, recovery), and by the end of H1 it also became the foundation for the new capacity management workstream (CAP-48585) which aims to prevent capacity-shortfall incidents and right-size over-provisioned environments.

Technology Transformation through AI & Automation: Direction shifted from project execution to a BAU service model for batch and file transfer, with L0/L1 activities moving to GOCC and GBO and CAWLA becoming the standard batch platform. The GBO Japan Batch Transition moved from a directional statement in May to a locked executable plan in June, with a September pilot cutover, mid-July onsite deployment, and 24x7 staffing under the new `ETS-GBO-N-Asia` support group. Alerting on the Developer Experience dashboard was tightened to actionable signals only, with the dashboard carrying the full data surface and a daily summary email providing a one-page health view. An AI-assisted drafting workflow was built to help produce Rapid Recovery Plans for the remaining Gold applications, and a quality-check step was added so that generated drafts are reviewed before publishing.

Outstanding Colleague Experience: Non-production monitoring onboarding was initiated in March for roughly 200 Dev and Test environments. Philippines branch laptop monitoring reached operational state and Singapore expressed interest in the same model — an opportunity to reuse the Japan pattern once the Asia GOCC model settles in September.

Key Milestones by Month

**March (W11 – W13)** — Production go-live: 23 applications monitored in the first week, Ingenium set as the reference standard, Rapid Recovery workstream initiated with Rohina's team, and 16 URLs formally handed over to GOCC for production monitoring. Employee Experience Dashboard registration resumed. Non-production onboarding kicked off for ~200 Dev/Test environments. Epsilon 3-tier HA proof-of-technology proposed by Balaji.

**April (W15 – W17)** — Phase-1 PS-to-GOCC transition checklist established as the single source of truth. Employee Experience Dashboard reached 66 of 81 systems; Developer Experience reached 45 of 60 non-prod environments. Transition progressed to 22 of 65 applications. SRM incident was converted into a Rapid Recovery onboarding case rather than a one-off firefight. Alert routing and rapid-recovery enablement procedures were formalized.

**May (W19 – W22)** — AQA automation was locked onto the Asia Jenkins pipeline and Delta test cases were confirmed reusable for Epsilon migration testing. INC08574374 — a mass mis-send of SMS and LINE messages to about 70,000 customers — surfaced clear batch observability gaps and drove volume-anomaly and mis-send-runbook work items. The Ingenium Rapid Recovery Plan was completed as the first Gold-app plan on the mandatory template. Direction was set to shift Batch and MFT L0/L1 operations to GOCC and GBO, using non-prod as the testbed.

**June (W23 – W26)** — Rapid Recovery hit the June 10 restart-sequence milestone with the Ingenium Gold plan delivered against the standard template; the plan was then published as a KB article. INC08624117 (Ingenium server freeze on June 8) drove a new rule: every plan must contain a server-restart authorization decision matrix. The R2R-driven push to onboard applications to ADX central logging was parked and replaced with an app-owner responsibility model; Asia ownership for ADX log monitoring was picked up by GOCC Middleware Operations. The September move to a single unified GOCC operations team (no L1/L2 silos) was locked. The GBO Japan Batch Transition moved from directional intent to an executable plan with a September pilot cutover.

**July (W27)** — CAP-48585 (Capacity Management) was formally adopted as a Lapu-Lapu workstream, targeting close by end of September, with Ingenium, NDM, and ServerF as pilot applications. A service account password expiry on June 27 broke ING APIM communication for SCV, MLK, PAW, and SSW; the post-incident review exposed a systemic silent-expiry pattern in Japan's Semi-Managed CyberArk accounts and opened a follow-on sweep across the rest of the estate.

Decisions Locked in H1

- **D002 (Mar 26)** — Directional GOCC delivery model for Japan monitoring: URL availability first, then APM performance, then Rapid Recovery. Application teams own logic and runbooks; GOCC owns standardized monitoring and first response.
- **D003 (Mar 17)** — Simplify patching so GOCC can execute independently; target weekday patching as the norm.
- **D004 (Mar 30)** — Standardize Japan incident documentation with CI-linked, searchable records.
- **D005 (Apr 16)** — Phase-1 checklist and impact-based alerting are the authoritative controls for the PS-to-GOCC transition.
- **D007 (Apr 23)** — Escalate non-standard monitoring applications to the owning team rather than building one-off workarounds.
- **D009 (Apr 24)** — SRM-class incidents require explicit validation steps and enforceable restart / recovery paths; close the gaps via Rapid Recovery onboarding.
- **D010 (May 11)** — AQA automation scripts onboard into the Asia Jenkins pipeline; Delta test cases are reused for Epsilon migration testing.
- **D011 (May 26)** — All Rapid Recovery Plans use the mandatory standard template; no self-publishing — everything routes through Thabani for KB publishing.
- **D012 (May 28)** — Shift Batch and MFT L0/L1 operations to GOCC (infrastructure and file transfer) and GBO (batch execution). Standardize batch execution on CAWLA. Use non-prod as the testbed.
- **D013 (Jun 9)** — Trim Developer Experience Dashboard alerting to actionable signals only; dashboard carries the full data surface; daily summary email is the one-page health view.
- **D014 (Jun 9)** — Shared-folder ACL compliance monitoring is in Lapu-Lapu scope; file transfer and batch remain separate.
- **D015 (Jun 9)** — Every Rapid Recovery Plan must include a server-restart authorization decision matrix.
- **D016 (Jun 16)** — Park the R2R-scope ADX onboarding push; central logging becomes an application-owner responsibility, re-engaged only when a specific incident demands it.
- **D017 (Jun 18)** — In September, GOCC transitions to a unified operations team without L1/L2 silos. Japan-market runbooks are retained until cutover, then updated.
- **D018 (Jun 24)** — The GBO Japan Batch Transition executable plan, operating principles, and September pilot timeline are locked.
- **D019 (Jul 1)** — Adopt the CAP-48585 capacity-management delivery plan as the Lapu-Lapu execution vehicle, targeting close by end of September.

Workstream Rundown

Production Monitoring (Employee XP / Business Capability): Went live in W11 with 23 applications and Ingenium as the reference standard. Dashboard coverage expanded through Q2, moved from draft to shared with the business, and by end of H1 was being asked for by Sales and other business stakeholders. Ito-san's request for a business-friendly external health-check report is the next refinement cycle.

Developer Experience Dashboard: Draft complete in early Q2, then tightened through Q2 to route only actionable signals as alerts, with the full data surface staying in the dashboard and a daily summary email providing a one-page view. Active hours are set to 9am–9pm JST. Enginium non-prod URL monitoring is still waiting on a firewall approval for the new Azure jump switch.

CMDB Mapping: Continuous cleanup by Yegor across H1. CMDB accuracy is the substrate for GOCC Transition, MMM L2, Patching, Rapid Recovery, and — as of end of H1 — the new capacity management workstream.

GOCC Transition: 25 of 65 applications onboarded (6 Gold and 19 Silver), enabling managed L0-L1 triage. Progress stalled at that number for several weeks in Q2, held up by PS Team engagement during their contract transition. The September move to a unified GOCC operations team (no L1/L2 silos, D017) is now locked, and a parallel 24x7 batch ownership track under GBO (D018) has been added on top.

MMM L2: Checklist for Japan's 6 Gold applications progressed through Q2; Ingenium data collected by Harish. Remaining Gold-app updates are still waiting on PS Team resource assignment. Capacity thresholds from CAP-48585 will now be added into the MMM L2 dashboards.

Patching: Standardization and weekday patch registration meetings have been running through Q2. Weekday patching for non-Gold, non-batch apps and servers is the target for reducing weekend workload. Ongoing risk: the team lacks the CMDB and ServiceNow query skills to drive standardized patching changes on their own.

Rapid Recovery: Went from workstream kick-off in March to first delivered Gold-app plan (Ingenium) on June 10, to a published KB article, to an AI-assisted drafting workflow with a quality-check step for the remaining Gold applications. Server-restart authorization matrix is now mandatory in every plan (D015). Extension to the remaining Gold applications still requires PS Team coordination.

ADX Registration: Started H1 as an R2R-scope push blocked on the COUE team, then shifted to an app-owner responsibility model (D016). Asia ownership for ADX log monitoring and audit was picked up by GOCC Middleware Operations. The first app-driven re-engagement is Godfrey Esguerra's CAWLA batch tracelog dashboard on the APROD ADX environment.

Batch / MFT: Direction set in May to move L0/L1 operations to GOCC and GBO with CAWLA as the standard batch platform (D012). In June the GBO Japan Batch Transition became an executable plan (D018) with a September pilot cutover, a July onsite deployment, and 24x7 staffing under the `ETS-GBO-N-Asia` support group. Open blockers at end of H1: completing the inventory of non-CA and manual jobs, PS Team engagement to author the batch plans, and pilot application selection.

Branch / JPE-JPW: Non-prod VNet complete with a small post-provisioning fix pending; production VNet still blocked on the subscription-level networking issue; 10 Japan branch laptops in service; Philippines branch laptop monitoring stood up in Q2 with the dashboard build still in progress; Singapore has expressed interest in the same pattern.

Epsilon (Ingenium 3-Tier HA POT): Proof-of-technology proposed in W13; AQA automation onboarding into Jenkins and Delta test-case reuse for Epsilon migration testing locked in W20 (D010).

Capacity Management (CAP-48585): New workstream adopted at the end of H1 (D019) to establish a repeatable process to estimate future capacity, prevent capacity-shortfall incidents, and right-size over-provisioned environments. Six work packages split between GOCC (baseline, forecast, thresholds, ongoing ops) and ETS (demand drivers, reporting). Pilot on Ingenium, NDM, and ServerF; target close end of September.

Credential Lifecycle Governance: New workstream opened at the end of H1 in response to the June 27 service account expiry. Formalize the rotation cadence for Semi-Managed accounts with CyberArk operations, fix the pre-expiry notification gap, register the failure mode in the affected Rapid Recovery Plans, and sweep the rest of the Japan Semi-Managed estate for silent-expiry and stale-owner exposure.

Notable Incidents

- **INC08574374 (May)** — Mass mis-send of SMS and LINE messages to about 70,000 customers. Surfaced batch observability gaps; drove work on volume-anomaly detection, circuit breakers, and a mis-send runbook.
- **INC08624117 (Jun 8)** — Ingenium server freeze; resolved by restart. Red Hat root-cause investigation still open at end of H1. Drove D015 — the mandatory server-restart authorization decision matrix in every Rapid Recovery Plan.
- **INC08672078 / PRB00024864 (Jun 27)** — `mfcgd\wasAPIMprod` service account password expired and broke ING APIM communication for SCV, MLK, PAW, and SSW; GAM reset restored service the same morning. Post-incident review found no rotation since May 2024, no accountable owner on record, and no pre-expiry notification configured — a silent-expiry pattern that is likely present in other Japan Semi-Managed accounts.

Standing Risks at End of H1

- **PS Team contract transition** is the single biggest constraint on throughput. It is holding up MMM L2 Gold-app updates, Rapid Recovery author assignment for the remaining Gold applications, and per-application onboarding to GOCC. Mitigation: Harish to Hirooka-san escalation, weekly per-app blocker tracking.
- **GOCC onboarding velocity** stalled at 25 of 65 applications for several weeks in Q2. Unblocking depends on the PS Team engagement above and on the September unified-operating-model cutover.
- **CMDB data quality** remains a named dependency for every downstream workstream and is now also a prerequisite for capacity management.
- **Patching team skills gap** — the team is not yet self-sufficient in querying CMDB / ServiceNow to drive standardized patching changes.
- **Silent-expiry exposure on Semi-Managed service accounts** — the June 27 incident is very likely not isolated; the sweep under T144 will confirm the size of the exposure.
- **INC08624117 root cause** still with Red Hat.
